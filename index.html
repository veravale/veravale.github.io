The work

Professional enough to be useful. Personal enough to tell the truth.

My practice sits at the edge of education, relational coaching, erotic literacy, and ritual. I have spent years moderating private salons, guiding couple conversations, writing curriculum for consent-based fantasy groups, and helping men name desires they were taught to bury.

I am not a clinical therapist and I do not present this as medical or mental health treatment. Clients come here for structured conversation, discernment, communication planning, fantasy integration, and, sometimes, the kind of velvet-gloved accountability that makes a man sit up straighter.

I do not chase. The calendar opens after the arrangement is settled; until then we can keep the conversation light, civil, and brief.
