Reading room

Notes on the psychology of the forbidden, written across three imperfect years.

These are essays, not doctrine. Some are polished. A few still carry the mood of the week they were written in.

April 17, 2026

When the fantasy is not about losing her

Many men first describe cuckolding as fear: “I keep imagining her with someone else.” After a few careful questions, the fear often opens into a more precise wish: to witness her sovereignty, to be chosen after comparison, to feel small without being discarded.

The useful question is not “Is this normal?” but “What does the scene repair, dramatize, or reveal?” That question has saved more couples than any rush to perform the fantasy.

February 9, 2026

Hotwifing requires logistics, not just courage

A couple can be emotionally brave and operationally careless. Calendars, testing cadence, sober check-ins, veto language, and re-entry rituals sound unromantic until they protect the erotic charge from panic.

I ask clients to decide in advance what happens the morning after. Breakfast is underrated aftercare. So is being held without having to be impressive.

November 21, 2025

Race, projection, and the interracial charge

Interracial fantasy can hold beauty, danger, admiration, taboo, shame, and lazy stereotype all at once. Adults have to separate embodied desire from inherited scripts. This does not mean sterilizing fantasy. It means refusing to make another person carry a cartoon.

When the charge depends on dehumanizing someone, slow down. When the charge comes from awe, contrast, history, and the ache of comparison, there may be room for honesty.

August 6, 2025

The devoted man is not weak

I used to bristle when men called themselves weak for wanting instruction. Now I listen for the difference between collapse and devotion. Collapse asks a woman to become a parent. Devotion offers attention, discipline, and reverence.

The former is exhausting. The latter can be delicious.

May 30, 2025

Why humiliation needs tenderness nearby

Humiliation play without tenderness curdles into punishment. Tenderness without edge can become theatre with no pulse. The art is in dosing: what gets said, who says it, when it stops, and how the man is welcomed back into ordinary daylight.

Good erotic cruelty is precise. Bad cruelty is vague and leaks everywhere.

January 14, 2025

Jealousy as evidence of attachment

Jealousy is not always a sign that something is wrong. Sometimes it is the body admitting, loudly, that the bond matters. The work is to keep jealousy from becoming a weapon while still letting it speak.

I have changed my mind here. I once tried to calm jealousy too quickly. Now I ask it to sit at the table and use its manners.

October 3, 2024

The first conversation should be almost boring

If your first disclosure is a cinematic confession, your partner may hear threat before desire. Start smaller. “There is a fantasy I want to understand better before I ask anything of you” is less glamorous and far more generous.

Some men want the scene so badly they skip the invitation. That is where many doors close.

June 18, 2024

Comparison is not always degradation

Comparison can humiliate, yes. It can also clarify. A man may discover he wants to admire another man through his wife’s pleasure, not disappear beneath him. That distinction changes the whole room.

Language matters. “Better” is a blunt instrument. “Different” often tells the truth with more heat.

February 27, 2024

Fantasy is not a contract

Arousal can make a possibility feel like a promise. It is not. Couples need permission to explore, revise, pause, and even retreat without calling the whole thing a failure.

The most resilient erotic lives I have seen are not fearless. They are editable.

September 12, 2023

What men mean when they ask to be “made useful”

Sometimes service is not about chores. It is about relief from the exhausting project of being central. A man may crave a role with rules because rules quiet the bargaining voice inside him.

Usefulness can be erotic when it is chosen. It becomes resentment when it is assumed.
