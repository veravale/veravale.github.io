Reading room

Essays for men who want the truth to keep its lipstick on.

These pieces are educational essays, field notes, and occasional provocations from years of private conversations. They are not clinical treatment, and they are not a substitute for medical or mental health care. They are meant to make desire less frantic, more literate, and a little more beautifully behaved.

April 17, 2026

When the fantasy is not about losing her

Many men first describe cuckolding as if they are confessing a threat. The first sentence usually sounds like panic: I cannot stop imagining her with another man. I am afraid I want it. I am afraid I will hate it. I am afraid she will like it too much. The fantasy arrives dressed as disaster because that is the only costume some men were ever given for longing.

But after a few careful questions, the center of the fantasy often shifts. It is not always about losing her. Sometimes it is about seeing her unedited. Sometimes it is about watching a woman stop managing her radiance for the comfort of a man who loves her. Sometimes it is about being returned to his place, not discarded, but placed: lower, quieter, more attentive, more useful. There can be relief in that, if everyone involved understands the difference between a scene and a sentence.

The men who suffer most are the ones who think the fantasy has only one meaning. They assume wanting comparison means they are inadequate. They assume wanting humiliation means they are broken. They assume wanting to witness means they are less masculine than the man imagined in the room. I rarely find those assumptions useful. They are too flat. Desire is rarely a receipt with one item on it.

A better question is: what does the image organize? Does it organize fear of abandonment, or hunger for devotion? Does it ask to be punished, or does it ask to be relieved of pretending to be in charge? Does it eroticize her autonomy because he trusts her, or because he secretly believes desire must be stolen to be real? These distinctions matter. They are the difference between building a ritual and lighting a match in a dry field.

I sometimes ask a man to describe the moment after. Not the charged moment, not the cinematic one, not the one with his pulse in his throat. After. Where is she? Where is he? Does she touch his face? Does he feel proud, emptied, angry, tender, obedient, frightened, clean? The after-picture tells me more than the fantasy itself. A man who imagines being held afterward is asking a different question than a man who imagines being ignored. Neither is automatically wrong. But they require different care.

A fantasy is not evidence of failure. It is evidence that the psyche is trying to make a theatre for something it cannot yet say plainly.

Hotwifing requires logistics, not just courage

Hotwifing is often sold in fantasy as a single act of boldness. She gets dressed. He watches. A door closes or opens. Someone becomes brave. That makes for a clean story, and clean stories are excellent at hiding the work. In real life, the couples who last are not the ones with the most theatrical courage. They are the ones who can talk about calendars without losing the heat.

Logistics are erotic when they protect the room. I mean that. A testing cadence can be a form of devotion. A sober check-in can be a kind of foreplay because it says: I care enough not to let arousal make all the decisions. A shared plan for getting home, for re-entry, for sleep, for the first morning after, is not bureaucracy. It is the trellis the vine climbs.

The couples who resist logistics usually tell me they do not want to kill the mood. What kills the mood is not preparation. What kills the mood is a panicked text at 1:14 in the morning because nobody agreed on whether silence meant privacy or neglect. What kills the mood is a husband trying to be noble while quietly drowning. What kills the mood is a wife feeling like she must manage his collapse instead of inhabiting her own pleasure.

I like agreements that are plain enough to remember when the body is loud. What words mean stop? What words mean slow down? What words mean I am jealous but not unsafe? Who gets details, and when? Are photos part of the arrangement or poison for later? Is the husband asking to be told everything because it nourishes him, or because anxiety is wearing a velvet mask and calling itself honesty?

And yes, I ask about breakfast. I am almost boring on this point. The morning after is where many couples discover what they actually made. A husband may need to be touched before being teased. A wife may need to be admired without being interrogated. The domestic world has to welcome the erotic world back in, or the erotic world becomes a glittering little exile.

Good planning does not make hotwifing tame. It makes it survivable enough to become more than one dazzling mistake. It lets the woman enjoy being wanted without becoming the household emergency. It lets the man taste his own jealousy without making her mop the floor afterward. That is not less spicy. It is grown-up spice, the kind that lingers.

Race, projection, and the interracial charge

Interracial fantasy can be one of the most charged territories I discuss, and it deserves more than either scolding or indulgence. The charge may contain admiration, taboo, history, contrast, curiosity, shame, social fear, beauty, and an old inherited ugliness that nobody invited but many people absorbed. Adults do not become ethical by pretending those layers are not there. They become ethical by learning which layer is speaking.

Some people want permission to keep every fantasy exactly as it first arrived. I do not offer that. First drafts often contain debris. Arousal is not a moral laundering machine. If a fantasy depends on making another person into a symbol with no interior life, it needs to be interrupted. Not shamed into secrecy, perhaps, but interrupted. There is no maturity in asking a real person to carry a cardboard role because the taboo once made the body light up.

At the same time, I distrust the impulse to sterilize desire until it resembles a policy document. Bodies notice difference. Culture marks difference. Power collects around difference. Sometimes the erotic imagination turns toward the place where the rules have been most heavily guarded. That does not automatically make the person vile. It does mean they must be careful, specific, and willing to be embarrassed without getting defensive.

When I work with a man drawn to interracial comparison, I ask what exactly is being compared. Is it imagined physicality? Social status? Confidence? Otherness? Is the fantasy about his wife choosing beyond his category, or about him being forced to admire someone he has secretly envied? Is he using race as a shortcut for dominance because he lacks a more honest vocabulary? The answer changes everything.

The more useful path is usually away from stereotype and toward specificity. A person is not a category with hands. A fantasy can hold difference without flattening the person who embodies it. The erotic mind can say: I am stirred by contrast, by history, by the fact that my body knows this is socially loaded. It does not need to say: this human exists to complete my humiliating script.

I have become stricter here over time, and kinder. Stricter because projection harms people. Kinder because shame rarely teaches nuance; it mostly teaches hiding. The goal is not to bleach desire. The goal is to make it accountable enough to be shared without turning anyone into furniture in someone else's theatre.

The devoted man is not weak

I used to flinch when men called themselves weak for wanting instruction. I heard the word and imagined collapse: a man asking a woman to make every decision, soothe every feeling, interpret every silence, and then call his helplessness devotion. That kind of dynamic is not erotic to me. It is unpaid management with better lighting.

But I have learned to listen more carefully. There is a difference between a man who wants to disappear and a man who wants to place his attention somewhere worthy. Devotion is not passivity. Devotion has posture. It remembers what was requested. It notices what is needed without making a parade of noticing. It can kneel in spirit while still paying the invoice, washing the glasses, scheduling the appointment, and owning its choices.

The devoted man is often tired of the crude performance of dominance he was told to wear. He does not necessarily want to be humiliated into nothing. He wants the relief of a hierarchy that has been chosen, named, and cared for. He wants to stop negotiating every inch of his importance. He wants a woman to look at him with that calm, dangerous certainty and say: here is your place tonight.

There is nothing weak about tolerating that charge without becoming silly. In fact, many men cannot. They want the feeling but not the discipline. They want the thrill of being corrected but not the humility of actually changing. They want a mistress voice to decorate the same old self. Devotion asks more than arousal. It asks a man to become reliable under pressure.

My private measure is simple: does his devotion make the woman freer? If yes, I become interested. If his submission gives her more space to breathe, more room to enjoy herself, more confidence that she will not be punished later for being powerful, then we may have something lovely. If his submission creates another job for her, then we have merely dressed entitlement in a collar.

A devoted man is not weak. A devoted man is arranged. And when he is arranged well, there is a particular shine to him. Quiet. Alert. A little ruined in the best way. Useful, not because he was diminished, but because he finally stopped demanding to be the center of every room he entered.

Why humiliation needs tenderness nearby

Humiliation is a sharp instrument. In the right hands, with the right agreements, it can reveal a hidden devotion, drain poison from shame, and turn a man's anxious pride into something softer and more available. In careless hands, it becomes vague cruelty with a better outfit. The distinction is not aesthetic. It is structural.

Good humiliation is specific. It knows what it is touching. It knows whether the man wants comparison, chastening, exposure, service, denial, or the delicious little sting of being seen too accurately. Bad humiliation is lazy. It throws insults at the wall and calls the bruises chemistry. I have very little patience for that. Precision is kinder, and frankly, more wicked.

Tenderness must be nearby because humiliation alters a person's sense of place. That is the point, often. He is lowered, teased, corrected, made to blush, made to feel the heat of not being the most impressive man in the room. But if there is no path back to belonging, the scene becomes exile. The body may still respond; bodies are not always wise. The relationship, however, keeps score.

I ask couples to decide how return will happen before descent begins. Will she touch his shoulder? Will she use his name? Will there be a phrase that means the sting is over and the bond is intact? Will he be expected to speak, or is silence part of his recovery? These details sound small until they prevent a long, cold drive home.

Tenderness does not weaken humiliation. It frames it. A woman can be merciless inside a well-held agreement precisely because the agreement has edges. A man can let himself be made small because small does not mean unsafe. This is why the best scenes often have a strange sweetness at the center. Not sentimental sweetness. Something darker and more adult: I can show you this part of me, and you will not abandon me to it.

I have changed one thing in my guidance over the years. I no longer ask whether humiliation is healthy in the abstract. The abstract answer is useless. I ask whether this humiliation, in this relationship, with these words, these histories, these limits, and this aftercare, increases honesty or teaches someone to endure contempt. One is art. The other is rot.

Jealousy as evidence of attachment

I used to rush jealousy out of the room. I thought I was being wise. A man would say he felt sick imagining his wife desired by someone else, and I would move quickly toward regulation, reassurance, breath, interpretation. Those are useful tools, but sometimes I was too quick. I treated jealousy like smoke when it was also a signal fire.

Jealousy is not always evidence that something is wrong. Sometimes it is the body admitting, with no manners at all, that the bond matters. It says: this person is not interchangeable. It says: I have placed part of myself here. It says: there is risk in wanting, and I would like everyone to stop pretending otherwise. That does not make jealousy noble. It makes it information.

The work is to keep jealousy from becoming a weapon while still letting it speak. A jealous man can become controlling very quickly if he believes the feeling grants him authority over another person's body, friendships, clothing, tone, or joy. That is the spoiled version of jealousy. It takes a real attachment and uses it as a leash. Nobody should romanticize that.

But a jealous man can also become more honest. He can say: I want this and I am frightened. I want to witness and I also want proof that I am not being erased. I want her freedom, but my nervous system is not yet elegant about it. There is dignity in that sentence. It does not demand obedience. It asks for collaboration.

In cuckolding and hotwifing conversations, jealousy often becomes the hidden third participant. Couples pretend they are negotiating acts when they are really negotiating the meaning of attachment under pressure. Will jealousy be mocked, worshipped, managed, eroticized, or listened to? Each choice builds a different marriage.

Now, when jealousy arrives, I ask it to sit at the table and use its manners. It does not get to drive. It does get to testify. Sometimes its testimony is childish. Sometimes it is exquisitely accurate. A couple who can tell the difference has more options than a couple who only knows how to suppress it or obey it.
