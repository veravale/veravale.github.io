Personal journal

The years of language.

These posts come from the stretch where private curiosity began turning into a shared practice. I was still trying to make everything sound cleaner than it felt.

January 27, 2023

Private does not mean pretend

A client asked whether a fantasy counts if it remains mostly in conversation. I answered him, then heard myself. That happens often in this work. I will say something measured to someone else and then have to sit with the fact that my own house has been listening.

Some people collect experiences because they are afraid their inner life is not real until it has witnesses. I understand that hunger. There is a particular panic in being vivid only to yourself. The mind begins to ask for proof: a scene, a confession, a body in the room, a calendar entry, a consequence.

Still, there are marriages where the private ritual is the thing, not a warm-up for the thing. A sentence repeated in the right voice can rearrange a man for days. A wife can become more sovereign without ever making spectacle of her sovereignty. A husband can learn devotion at the edge of possibility rather than in the middle of an event.

I am less impatient with privacy now. Not every door must open to prove it exists. Some doors are powerful because both people know exactly where they are and choose, for now, to let the handle remain warm.

September 8, 2022

What changed after I stopped managing his face

I used to watch his expression too closely. One crease in his forehead and I would soften, edit, retreat. It looked loving. It was also a way of keeping myself safe from his full response.

Women are praised for this kind of vigilance. We call it empathy, intuition, emotional intelligence. Sometimes it is those things. Sometimes it is a habit of taking responsibility for every ripple in the room because the alternative would be letting someone else have an honest reaction.

He asked me to stop translating him before he spoke. I was offended for a whole afternoon because he was right. I had been deciding too quickly what his silence meant. Shame, threat, refusal, overwhelm. I had a whole internal committee, very efficient and completely unauthorized.

When I stopped managing his face, our conversations became slower and better. He had room to discover whether jealousy was actually jealousy or simply surprise. I had room to discover that his discomfort did not always require my retreat. We both learned that a furrowed brow is not a verdict.

There is a form of dominance in letting another adult have his own reaction without rushing to rescue yourself from it. I did not know that then. I know it now.

June 14, 2022

A salon note I brought home in my coat pocket

At a small salon that June, a woman said she wanted her husband to stop asking for permission in a voice that made refusal impossible. The room went quiet because everyone recognized the shape of it. Not coercion exactly. Not innocence either.

On the train home I wrote one sentence on the back of a receipt: “Submission can manipulate when it arrives as helplessness.” I did not like the sentence. It was too sharp. It also named something I had been seeing in clients and, uncomfortably, in my own marriage.

My husband is not helpless. But he can perform uncertainty beautifully when he wants me to lead without having to admit that he has asked. I can perform competence beautifully when I want control without having to admit I enjoy it. We are a talented pair of cowards when we want to be.

That night we talked about clean asking. Not fancy asking. Not perfect asking. Clean. “I want your authority here.” “I want to feel compared, but not mocked tonight.” “I want you to decide, and I know that is still a request.” It was awkward at first. Then it became liberating.

People imagine erotic clarity will sound elegant. Often it sounds like two adults learning not to smuggle their wishes into the room under a blanket.

March 19, 2022

The race conversation I was not ready for

This was the year I stopped congratulating myself for being “careful” and started asking whether careful had become avoidant. Interracial fantasy can be intimate, charged, admiring, messy, historically loaded, and lazy in the same breath. We had used some borrowed language because borrowed language is easy when you are nervous.

We spent one Saturday pulling apart what actually had heat for us: contrast, comparison, admiration, the fear of not being enough, the terrible thrill of being seen next to someone else and still chosen afterward. Some of the old words lost their charge once we looked at them. Others became more precise.

I remember saying, too quickly, “We are not those people.” He asked, “Which people?” and I became cross because I had no clean answer. I meant careless people. I meant crude people. I meant people who let fantasy borrow from history without paying attention. But I also meant people who were not as morally polished as I wanted to imagine us.

That conversation humbled me. Not in a performative way. In the private way where you make dinner afterward and keep thinking about a sentence while chopping onions. I do not trust desire less now. I trust slogans less. Desire can be intelligent, but only if the adults holding it refuse to become lazy just because they are aroused.

December 2, 2021

A note I nearly deleted

I like being wanted by my husband. I also like him knowing that his wanting does not own the room.

There. I wrote it. Then I made tea, came back, and nearly deleted it because it sounded too sharp. I imagined a reader deciding I was cruel. Worse, I imagined a reader deciding I was pretending to be cruel because I was afraid to be plain.

The sentence stayed because it was true. His desire matters to me. It warms me, flatters me, steadies me. It is one of the great honors of my life to be wanted over time by someone who has seen me in ugly pajamas and in grief and in triumph and in the small daily failures no one writes poems about.

But his wanting cannot be the border of my selfhood. When women forget this, they call it love until the resentment starts speaking through locked jaws. When men forget this, they call it devotion until devotion begins asking for receipts.

I did not yet have the courage to publish the entry then. I saved it as “December note, maybe too much.” It was not too much. It was the beginning of a cleaner tenderness.

May 31, 2021

The first time I let authority feel beautiful

I had spent years telling women that desire is allowed to have posture. Then I kept trying to make my own authority sound like a service announcement: polite, helpful, neutral. I was not afraid of authority in theory. I was afraid of the pleasure I took in it.

One night he asked me to choose the terms of a conversation. Not the outcome, not some grand arrangement, just the terms. Where he sat. What he could ask. When he had to stop explaining and simply listen. The request was mild on paper. In the room it had a pulse.

I expected to feel cruel. Instead I felt clear. That difference changed something in me. Clarity can be a kind of mercy. It tells everyone where the walls are, where the door is, which parts of the room are ceremony and which parts are simply marriage.

Afterward he said he felt relieved, which surprised me. I had imagined he would feel diminished. He said no, being asked to follow carefully made him feel trusted. That is still one of the loveliest things he has ever given me: the understanding that authority, when chosen well, can be a form of trust rather than a theft of it.

Sometimes command is tenderness with its shoulders back. I have used that sentence so often now that it sounds polished. It was not polished when I learned it. It was a little frightening, and I was wearing a faded cardigan.

February 14, 2021

Valentine's Day, badly timed

We attempted a serious conversation because the calendar was sentimental and we were idiots.

It went poorly. Not catastrophically, just poorly. I wanted reverence; he wanted reassurance; neither of us admitted the plain need underneath. We had dressed the evening in expectation and then became irritated when the room did not behave like a scene.

I remember the exact failure. He asked whether a particular fantasy still interested me. I heard pressure where he meant hope. I answered with analysis where he needed warmth. Then he became careful, which made me feel managed, which made me sharper. Very elegant, all of it. Two people with excellent vocabulary failing at the basics.

We salvaged the evening with takeout and a rule that important erotic talks do not have to happen on holidays. Especially not holidays that already arrive carrying jewelry ads, restaurant menus, and absurd little expectations about what desire should look like under a reservation time.

I am fond of that failure now. It taught us that charged conversation needs ordinary conditions more than decorative ones. A Tuesday with clean sheets and no agenda has saved us more than any candle ever did.

October 7, 2020

Consent paperwork, candlelight, and other unromantic magic

I made a document. Of course I did. Boundaries, language, pause signals, health expectations, emotional weather, what we would never use against each other in an argument. He laughed at the table of contents, then asked for a section on re-entry.

That was when I understood that structure does not kill heat. Structure lets heat be brave. It gives the dangerous thing a rail to hold. I had known this professionally, but knowing something professionally is different from sitting beside your husband at a kitchen table while he asks what you will need if one of you feels unexpectedly tender afterward.

The first draft was dreadful. Too many categories. Too much cleverness. A line about “mutual sovereignty” that I still cannot read without wanting to apologize to the English language. But beneath the ugliness there was care. We were trying to make a container before pouring anything volatile into it.

I still have that document. It has been revised many times. The current version is shorter and less impressed with itself. It asks better questions: What do we do if one person wants to stop without explaining immediately? What language is invitation and what language is injury? What ordinary care happens the next morning?

People mock paperwork because they imagine erotic life should be spontaneous. I have found that spontaneity improves when everyone knows where the floor is.
